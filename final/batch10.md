# BATCH 10 — 300-Source Strategy Synthesis: Source-Backed Strategy Foundation for 2029/2030 Deep-Tech Launch

## TL;DR
- The corpus converges on one dominant cross-domain story: the **AI-compute/data-center power buildout** is the single largest 2029/2030 demand magnet, pulling along medium-voltage power electronics (SiC SST, 800VDC), advanced packaging (hybrid bonding/chiplets), long-duration storage, and thermal management — these are the highest-confidence opportunity zones because independent domains (EN, SC, EN-grid, CM) all point at the same 2027-2030 inflection.
- **China-first is credible and evidence-backed** for low-altitude economy, embodied AI/humanoids, and 3D-integration equipment (15FYP self-reliance + Big Fund III + NDRC/MIIT standards), while **US expansion is strongest** in grid/data-center power hardware, extreme-environment materials (DOE/national-lab pull), and FDA-cleared AI/ML device subsystems.
- Export-control reality as of mid-2026 is a **moving target favoring China-domestic supply chains**: BIS shifted H200/MI325X to case-by-case licensing (Jan 15 2026) but with 25% tariff, 50% volume cap, and congressional backlash — so any compute-adjacent venture must assume policy volatility and design for China-domestic or export-control-agnostic positioning.

## Key Findings

### Reconciliation 1 — Global data-center electricity demand to 2030
The corpus figure of ~945 TWh by 2030 is **confirmed as the IEA Base Case headline number** from the April 2025 *Energy and AI* report: data centres consumed ~415 TWh (1.5% of global) in 2024 and are projected to "more than double to around 945 TWh by 2030." The US accounted for 45%, China 25%, Europe 15% of 2024 consumption. The "~950 TWh" figure that also appeared is from the IEA's **updated late-2025 *Key Questions on Energy and AI*** analysis, which restates the trajectory as "roughly doubling from 485 TWh in 2025 to 950 TWh in 2030." **Resolution: both are IEA; 945 TWh (Base Case, 2030, from 2024 baseline) is the canonical figure; 950 TWh is the late-2025 update from a 2025 baseline.** US+China account for ~80% of growth to 2030. (Tier 1 — IEA.)

### Reconciliation 2 — US data-center / battery capacity additions ("15 GW vs 18.2 GW")
The logged discrepancy is resolved against current EIA data: EIA's **February 2025 forecast projected 18.2 GW of utility-scale battery storage additions for 2025**, but the **actual realized 2025 figure was 15 GW** (per EIA's December 2025 Preliminary Monthly Electric Generator Inventory, which states developers plan 24 GW in 2026 "compared with a record 15 GW added in 2025"). **Resolution: 18.2 GW was the forecast; 15 GW was actual — the logged "15 GW actual vs 18.2 GW forecast" is correct.** Total 2025 capacity additions were ~53 GW (largest since 2002); 2026 is forecast at a record 86 GW. (Tier 1 — EIA.)

### Reconciliation 3 — BIS export-control status (H200/MI325X) as of mid-2026
Confirmed and updated: on **January 15, 2026, BIS published a final rule (Federal Register 2026-00789)** shifting H200 and AMD MI325X exports to China/Macau from "presumption of denial" to **case-by-case review**, for chips with Total Processing Performance (TPP) <21,000 and total DRAM bandwidth <6,500 GB/s. Conditions: 25% import tariff, ≤50% volume cap vs US sales, US third-party testing, KYC. This implements Trump's Dec 8 2025 announcement. **However, as of mid-2026 the policy is contested**: a State Department review has reportedly stalled shipments, the House Foreign Affairs Committee voted 42-2 to advance the AI OVERWATCH Act (would ban Blackwell-class sales to China for two years and give Congress veto over future licenses), and reexport/transfer applications remain under presumption of denial. (Tier 1 — Federal Register, BIS; Tier 2 — law-firm and policy analyses.)

## Details

### Deliverable 1 — Executive synthesis: the cross-domain 2029/2030 story
The corpus tells a coherent story: **AI compute demand is the gravitational center of the 2026-2030 deep-tech economy**, and it radiates outward into four hard-engineering bottlenecks where small teams can build defensible niche products: (1) **grid-to-rack power delivery** (MV SiC power electronics, SST, 800VDC, active-decoupling buffers); (2) **advanced packaging and its metrology** (hybrid bonding, chiplets, sub-50nm alignment, thermal characterization); (3) **firm/long-duration power and thermal** (LDES, geothermal, fuel cells, data-center cooling); and (4) **extreme-environment and extreme-precision components** (rad-hard, UHTC/RHEA, high-field magnets, metrology instruments). Surrounding this core, two China-policy-driven adjacencies — **low-altitude economy** and **embodied AI/humanoids** — offer China-first beachheads but carry softer demand evidence and overcapacity/safety risk. The medtech AI/ML lane is real and growing but is diagnostic/radiology-saturated (the founder-preference list explicitly warns against diagnostic overproduction), so the defensible angle is hardware subsystems and regulated SaMD-adjacent tooling, not another radiology algorithm.

### Deliverable 3 — Cross-domain timing convergence on 2029/2030
- **2026-2027 inflection (power architecture):** NVIDIA 800V production (mid-2026 air-cooled, 2027 Kyber); Vertiv/Eaton 800VDC product lines (2H2026); SST hyperscale deployments beginning (Delta Feb 2026). A subsystem prototyped 2026-2028 can hit a maturing market by 2029. (EN, SC.)
- **2027-2030 (packaging):** D2W hybrid bonding scaling to <2µm pitch; metrology is the persistent gap → a metrology/inspection or thermal-characterization niche is well-timed for 2029. (SC, CM.)
- **2030 (LDES + SMR + storage):** DOE cost targets land at 2030; first SMRs ~2030; data-center battery storage ~20-25 GW by 2030 (IEA). (EN.)
- **2026-2030 (China embodied AI + LAE):** 15FYP runs 2026-2030; humanoid standards (2026) and eVTOL certification cadence converge mid-plan. (CN, AI.)
- **Persistent/independent of AI cycle (extreme environments):** fusion PFC materials, rad-hard, high-field magnets mature on national-lab timelines — slower but AI-cycle-resilient. (EX.)

### Deliverable 4 — China-first opportunities AND risks
**Opportunities:** (a) 3D-integration/hybrid-bonding **equipment and metrology** — Big Fund III is explicitly funding this (Piotech/Tuojing Jianke) and it sidesteps EUV/leading-edge export controls; (b) **low-altitude economy enabling hardware** (not the aircraft) — drone/eVTOL powertrain, thermal, airspace-management subsystems riding the **70-80% NEV "three-electric" (battery/motor/electronic-control) supply-chain overlap** (per Xuancheng municipal government and eVTOL developer Wofei Changkong, which states the systems "can share about 70% of the supply chain"); (c) **embodied-AI components** (actuators, safety-rated sensing, power) into >140 humanoid makers; (d) **data-center power localization** as China builds out under "East Data West Compute."
**Risks:** (a) **overcapacity/involution (内卷)** — eVTOL and humanoid sectors show classic bubble signals (Jamestown; UBTech "half as efficient"); (b) **thin consumer demand** behind headline TAMs (LAE figures aggregate legacy GA); (c) **foreign-founder market-access and IP-localization pressure** under the "new national system"; (d) **military-civil fusion** dual-use exposure that can trigger US outbound-investment/Entity-List action; (e) policy-subsidy dependence rather than organic pain.

### Deliverable 5 — US expansion opportunities AND risks
**Opportunities:** (a) **grid-to-rack power hardware** (SST, 800VDC, MV power electronics) — DG Matrix, Heron Power, Amperesand show a forming but uncrowded market with utility/hyperscale pull (AEP/Bloom 1 GW; Duke/NextEra pilots); (b) **extreme-precision metrology and extreme-environment materials** with national-lab/DoD single-source demand (NRL SSTR, FAR 6.302; DOE fusion-materials programs); (c) **LDES balance-of-system and power-conversion**; (d) **FDA-cleared AI/ML device hardware subsystems** outside saturated radiology.
**Risks:** (a) **transformer/gas-turbine supply bottlenecks** — per Wood Mackenzie's *Untangling the US Transformer Supply Chain Crisis*, the **US faces a 30% shortfall in power transformers and 10% in distribution units in 2025**, with imports an estimated 80% of power-transformer supply and 50% of distribution supply (Senior Analyst Ben Boucher); power-transformer demand surged 116% since 2019, and large-unit lead times average 128 weeks (GSUs 144 weeks) — these can delay deployment-dependent revenue; (b) **grid interconnection queues**; (c) **export-control whipsaw** if a product touches advanced compute; (d) **CHIPS/SBIR funding-cycle dependence**; (e) hyperscaler buyer concentration → design-win-or-die dynamics.

### Deliverable 10 — Source-backed constraints for idea generation (guardrails)
1. **No standalone diagnostic/radiology AI** — FDA list shows 76% radiology saturation (1,104 of 1,451); preference list bans diagnostic overproduction.
2. **Treat all market-size/CAGR as context, not pain** — especially LAE's 1.5T-yuan figure (aggregates legacy sectors; Jamestown contrarian evidence) and SST/800VDC Tier-3 TAMs.
3. **Assume export-control volatility** — design China-domestic or control-agnostic; never assume H200-class access is stable.
4. **Avoid leading-edge cleanroom/EUV dependence** — aligns with reduced-cleanroom-burden preference; prefer packaging/metrology/power.
5. **Anchor to a named buyer** — every cluster must map to a CM-family buyer type (hyperscaler, OEM, lab, utility), not a TAM.
6. **Prefer 2026-2028 small-team-prototypable TRL** — exclude fusion PFC, 45T magnets, generative medical devices as core (too early).
7. **Don't cite vendor marketing as independent evidence** — label company claims (e.g., SST efficiency, NVIDIA copper savings).
8. **Hardware-software integration preferred** — power electronics + controls, robot safety + sensing, metrology + analytics.
