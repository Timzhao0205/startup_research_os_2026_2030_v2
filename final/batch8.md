# BATCH 08 — Customer Pain, Procurement, Market Structure & Public-Filing Evidence (Source Corpus, FINAL Batch)

## TL;DR
- Batch 08 logged **44 counted qualifying sources** (34 Tier 1 / 8 Tier 2 / 2 Tier 3 = **95.5% Tier 1/2**), clearing the 75% quality gate, plus 3 company-claim rows and multiple Tier-3/Tier-4 exclusions that do not count.
- Evidence is deliberately biased toward the venture's target sectors (semiconductors/advanced packaging, power electronics/wide-bandgap, energy-grid-data-center, AI/robotics/drones/low-altitude, biomedical instrumentation, aerospace/fusion) — with concrete customer-concentration percentages, named procurement vehicles and award values, backlog figures, and market-share/concentration data points.
- Assuming all Batch 08 counted sources are net-new, the running corpus moves from ~330 to **~374 counted toward 300**; Tier 1/2 share stays well above 90% and Tier 3 stays far under the 70 cap.

## Key Findings
1. **Customer concentration is documented and severe in target-sector public filers — this is fact, not inference.** Bloom Energy's FY2025 10-K (SEC EDGAR, be-20251231.htm) states verbatim: *"During the year ended December 31, 2025, revenue from three customers, the first of which is our related party… accounted for approximately 43%, 13% and 12% of our total revenue"* (vs. 23%/16%/14% in FY2024 — concentration is **rising**). Methode Electronics' FY2026 10-K (mei-20260502.htm): *"our five largest customers accounted for approximately 41% of our consolidated net sales. One customer represented more than 10% of our consolidated net sales at 10.9%."* Willdan's FY2025 10-K shows two energy customers (Clark County School District and Southern California Edison) at 27.4% of segment revenue with a $1.0B backlog.
2. **Procurement friction and vehicles are concretely mapped.** DIU's Commercial Solutions Opening (CSO) awards prototype Other Transactions (10 U.S.C. 4022) in 60–90 days using ≤5-page/15-slide briefs and converts to sole-source follow-on production via a no-recompete "Success Memo"; NRL ran a FAR 6.302 sole-source for wide-/ultrawide-bandgap thermal metrology; the Firefly VICTUS SOL launch was a $21.81M task order on the OSP-4 IDIQ; DOE fusion uses pay-on-completion milestone OTs. Automotive power-electronics entry is gated by AEC-Q100/Q101 and ISO 26262 ASIL qualification.
3. **Market structure is highly concentrated at chokepoints.** Per CSIS (Oct 2024): *"Besi holds 40 percent of the total global market for 'die attach' and 74 percent of the total global market for 'advanced die placement'"* — its competitors are ASMPT and Kulicke & Soffa. Per USCC (Nov 14, 2025): *"China-based equipment manufacturers met only 9.6 percent of domestic demand in 2023"* for 20–14nm chipmaking equipment. Per SIA 2025 Factbook: *"In 2024, semiconductor firms based in the U.S. held 50.4 percent of the total semiconductor market share"*, while China is *"24 percent of the total global market"* of demand.
4. **Demand-pull from data-center power is a Tier-1-evidenced buyer signal, but is demand/timing — NOT proof of product-specific pain.** EIA (Jan 13, 2026) forecasts the strongest four-year U.S. electricity-demand growth since 2000, driven by data centers; IEA reports data-center electricity rose 17% in 2025 and the SMR conditional-offtake pipeline grew from 25 GW (end-2024) to 45 GW.
5. **China customer segments and localization barriers are quantified** via NBS output data (equipment-manufacturing value-added +9.2%, industrial-robot output +20.6% YoY in 2025) and Rhodium/USCC Made-in-China-2025 assessments (biomedicine, high-end medical devices, and advanced semiconductors remain foreign-dependent; foreign firms hold ~83% share in high-performance medical devices per EUCCC).
