<task>
Perform a customer-pain and niche-wedge scan for high-end deep-tech markets.
</task>


<source_requirements>
Use Project files `01_sources/source_whitelist.md`, `01_sources/source_quality_policy.md`, and `01_sources/source_300_requirement.md`.
Prioritize Tier 1 and Tier 2 sources. Tier 3 may be used for market timing and triangulation. Tier 4 may be logged as weak signals but must not count toward the 300-source requirement.
Use domain-targeted `site:` queries where practical.
Do not cite company marketing as independent evidence; label it as company claim.
Do not treat market growth as customer pain.
For every major claim, cite sources and distinguish fact, inference, and speculation.
</source_requirements>


<research_questions>
1. Which customers have severe technical pain and high willingness to pay?
2. Which customers buy high-performance niche systems even at high prices?
3. Which problems are too small or weird for large incumbents but painful enough for a startup?
4. Which China-first customers could adopt earlier than US customers?
5. Which US customers could later validate premium quality, compliance, or exportable credibility?
6. Which markets have long sales cycles requiring customer discovery now?
</research_questions>

</customer_categories>
