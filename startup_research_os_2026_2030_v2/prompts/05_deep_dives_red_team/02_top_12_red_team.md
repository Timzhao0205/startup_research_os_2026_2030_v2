<task>
Act as a hostile technical diligence committee, export-control counsel, China/US policy analyst, medical/regulatory reviewer, and deep-tech investor. Red-team the top 12 startup ideas.
</task>


<source_requirements>
Use Project files `01_sources/source_whitelist.md`, `01_sources/source_quality_policy.md`, and `01_sources/source_300_requirement.md`.
Prioritize Tier 1 and Tier 2 sources. Tier 3 may be used for market timing and triangulation. Tier 4 may be logged as weak signals but must not count toward the 300-source requirement.
Use domain-targeted `site:` queries where practical.
Do not cite company marketing as independent evidence; label it as company claim.
Do not treat market growth as customer pain.
For every major claim, cite sources and distinguish fact, inference, and speculation.
</source_requirements>


<red_team_lenses>
For each idea, attack it from:
1. Technical infeasibility
2. Hidden capex
3. Slow customer adoption
4. Weak willingness to pay
5. Incumbent response
6. Commodity trap
7. China regulatory risk
8. US regulatory risk
9. Export-control risk
10. Data access risk
11. Supply-chain risk
12. IP freedom-to-operate risk
13. Founder-fit risk
14. 2029/2030 timing risk
15. Why a better-funded company would win
</red_team_lenses>

<deliverable>
For each idea:
- strongest argument for;
- strongest argument against;
- fatal-risk probability: low / medium / high;
- evidence gaps;
- must-test assumptions;
- cheapest experiment to test each assumption;
- kill criteria;
- continue / pause / kill decision.

End with a revised top 8.
</deliverable>
