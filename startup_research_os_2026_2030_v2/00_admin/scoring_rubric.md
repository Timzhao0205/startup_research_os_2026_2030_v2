# Startup Idea Scoring Rubric — 100 points

Use this rubric after the unbiased opportunity universe is built and filtered.

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Pain intensity and willingness to pay | 12 |
| High-end niche wedge quality | 10 |
| Standalone product value | 10 |
| Technical feasibility and TRL path by 2029 | 10 |
| 2026–2028 R&D pilot feasibility | 8 |
| Hardware/software integration depth | 8 |
| Defensibility and non-obvious know-how | 8 |
| China-first wedge quality | 8 |
| US expansion feasibility | 7 |
| Regulatory/export-control risk, inverted | 7 |
| Capital efficiency and low cleanroom dependency | 5 |
| Extreme-performance / order-of-magnitude potential | 4 |
| Founder fit after unbiased search | 3 |

## Required labels

For each idea, assign:

- Product type: standalone product / component / subsystem / platform / software-hardware service / diagnostic tool
- Cleanroom dependency: none / low / medium / high
- Prototype capex band: <$25k / $25k–$100k / $100k–$500k / $500k–$2M / >$2M
- Likely 2029 readiness: low / medium / high
- China regulatory/export sensitivity: low / medium / high
- US regulatory/export sensitivity: low / medium / high
- Sales-cycle difficulty: low / medium / high
- Clinical/regulatory approval requirement: none / low / medium / high
- Restricted chips/equipment/data dependency: none / low / medium / high
- Evidence strength: high / medium / low

## Score penalties

Reduce score when:

- evidence is weak;
- top claim depends on company marketing;
- top claim depends on Tier 4 sources;
- customer pain is inferred from market growth rather than direct workflow pain;
- product is actually consulting;
- cleanroom dependency is high;
- Stanford IP is required;
- China/US export controls make the strategy incoherent;
- the idea is mainly diagnostics/testing and not standalone functionality.
