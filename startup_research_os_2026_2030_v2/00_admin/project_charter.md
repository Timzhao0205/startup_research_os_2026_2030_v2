# Project Charter — Startup Research OS 2026–2030

## Mission

Build a rigorous, evidence-controlled research system to identify high-end niche startup opportunities suitable for 2029/2030 launch, with background research, technical scouting, customer discovery, and R&D pilots during 2026–2028.

## Founder context

The founder is an EE PhD student with broad hardware/software/deep-tech capabilities, including embedded systems, PCB/circuit/IC/FPGA/microcontroller work, controls, power electronics, sensors, simulation, CAD, device fabrication familiarity, and AI/software skills.

The founder's current PhD topic must not bias the opportunity search. Treat the PhD as a skillset, not as a market or IP constraint.

## Startup preference

Prefer opportunities with:

- standalone products, components, subsystems, platforms, or hardware/software systems;
- high-end niche wedge markets;
- China-first or China-early adoption with credible US expansion;
- extreme-performance or extreme-environment angle;
- acceptable TRL and pilotability before 2029;
- low founding-team cleanroom dependency;
- defensible system integration, performance, data, reliability, supply-chain, or regulatory wedge.

## Non-goals

- Do not generate generic "AI for X" ideas.
- Do not overproduce diagnostics, testing, characterization, inspection, or monitoring tools.
- Do not assume access to Stanford IP or patents.
- Do not assume market size equals customer pain.
- Do not rank an idea highly if sources are weak.
- Do not recommend an idea mainly because it resembles the founder's PhD.

## Evidence principle

No major strategic recommendation should be made until the source corpus contains at least 300 unique qualifying sources, with at least 230 Tier 1 or Tier 2 sources.

## Timeline

### 2026

Build source corpus, opportunity landscape, idea database, scoring methodology, and initial customer-discovery list.

### 2027

Run early prototypes, simulations, interviews, and technical/commercial risk tests across 3 primary tracks and 3 backup tracks.

### 2028

Reduce to 1–2 serious startup tracks; build stronger MVPs, supply-chain paths, pilot customer relationships, and regulatory/export-control clarity.

### 2029

Decide whether to incorporate, join an existing startup, license technology, defer, or pivot.

### 2030

Scale the first niche into adjacent high-end markets if initial wedge is validated.
