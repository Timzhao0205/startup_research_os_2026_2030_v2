# Policy-Trigger Watchlist Verification (as of 2026-06-25)

## TL;DR
- **Of the 8 triggers, none has reached its "fired" trigger state**: TRIG-001 (BVLOS final rule), TRIG-002 (AI OVERWATCH enacted), TRIG-004 (SiC/semiconductor tariff rate), TRIG-005 (NRL executed contract), TRIG-006 (powered da Vinci RCT), and TRIG-007 (near-term D2W hybrid-bonding pull) all remain UNFIRED; TRIG-003 (BIS chip rule) remains an active, in-force final rule (i.e., not rescinded/tightened — the watched *change* has not happened); TRIG-008 (FDA OoC) has advanced via new draft guidance and bill movement but created no binding qualification mandate.
- **The two most decision-relevant changes since your as-of facts**: (a) FDA issued a **March 18, 2026 draft NAM guidance** (FR 2026-05390; comments closed May 18, 2026) and the **FDA Modernization Act 3.0 advanced in the House (H.R. 2821)** after the Senate passed S.355 by unanimous consent on Dec 16, 2025; (b) the **AI OVERWATCH Act gained a Senate companion (S.4456, introduced May 4, 2026)** but neither chamber has passed it on the floor.
- **TRIG-005 is the weakest-sourced**: still only a sole-source Notice of Intent; no executed Navy contract is publicly verifiable in USAspending/FPDS/SAM. Keep on watch at low-to-medium confidence due to possible FPDS ingest lag.

## Key Findings
- BIS rule 2026-00789 is the only trigger sitting in an in-force "enacted/active" state; it remains effective and unrescinded — meaning the *watched event* (a rescission or tightening) has **not** occurred.
- Every legislative trigger (AI OVERWATCH Act; FDA Modernization Act 3.0) is mid-process — **none enacted**. Do not treat committee advancement or single-chamber passage as enactment.
- Both the drone (Part 108) and tariff (Section 301 SiC) triggers are confirmed pending, each with a concrete forward reference date but **no fired action**.
- The clinical (da Vinci RCT) and standards (HBM hybrid bonding) triggers show movement, but in the *opposite* direction for HBM (further delay) and short-of-threshold for the RCT (force/process endpoints, not powered patient outcomes).

### TRIG-003 — BIS rule 2026-00789 (advanced-computing / AI-chip exports to China/Macau)
(a) **Status:** In force and unchanged. The final rule (FR Doc 2026-00789), effective **Jan 15, 2026**, shifted license applications for H200/MI325X-equivalent chips (TPP <21,000 and total DRAM bandwidth <6,500 GB/s) from presumption of denial to **case-by-case review** for direct exports from the U.S. to China/Macau. **No rescission, amendment, tightening, or superseding 2026 rule** on advanced-computing exports to China/Macau was located as of 2026-06-25. (Reexports, in-country transfers, transfers to listed entities, and Blackwell-class chips remain under presumption of denial.)
(b) **Source:** Federal Register, "Revision to License Review Policy for Advanced Computing Commodities," FR Doc 2026-00789, Docket 260112-0028. https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2026/01/15/2026-00789/
(c) **Classification:** enacted (in-force final rule); the *watched change* (rescind/tighten) has NOT fired
(d) **Confidence:** high
(e) **Change vs as-of:** No change to the rule itself. Surrounding context: a paired 25% Section 232 tariff (presidential proclamation, effective Jan 15, 2026), the rule's public comment period reportedly closing in March 2026, and ongoing congressional pushback via the AI OVERWATCH Act (TRIG-002), which seeks to codify/tighten elements of this policy.
(f) **Next milestone:** BIS signaled periodic review of thresholds; no fixed date. Watch for amendments as Blackwell/MI400-class chips ramp and if AI OVERWATCH advances.

### TRIG-006 — da Vinci 5 force feedback UPGRADED by a powered clinical-outcomes RCT
(a) **Status:** NOT met. No **powered randomized clinical-outcomes** trial demonstrating patient-outcome benefit exists. Evidence remains preclinical/company-supported plus small retrospective/pilot work. The manufacturer claims force feedback reduces tissue force "by up to 43%"; the Cedars-Sinai RCT led by Dr. Andrew J. Hung (29 novice surgeons) found significantly lower suturing force (e.g., 1.71 N vs 2.40 N on bladder tissue, p<0.006) — but on ex vivo models, not patient outcomes. The one genuinely randomized registered trial, **ClinicalTrials.gov NCT07247175** (Samsung Medical Center; Prof. Seong Soo Jeon and Dr. Jiwoong Yu), enrolls 60 patients with two surgeons each performing 30 RARP cases with Force Feedback ON/OFF randomly assigned, but its **primary endpoints are (1) mean traction force and (2) total instrument path length during seminal vesicle dissection** — not a powered patient-outcomes design (patient-reported outcomes are secondary only). Intuitive's NCT06879912 is **observational** (200 patients, intra-operative force measurement).
(b) **Source:** ClinicalTrials.gov NCT07247175 (https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT07247175) and NCT06879912 (https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT06879912). Force-reduction figures: Intuitive/company-supported (Surg Endosc; Eur Urol Open Sci).
(c) **Classification:** speculative (trigger not fired; evidence preclinical/company-supported)
(d) **Confidence:** high
(e) **Change vs as-of:** Minor progress. A new *randomized* study (NCT07247175) appeared, but its design targets biomechanical/process metrics, not powered clinical outcomes — so the trigger threshold is still not met.
(f) **Next milestone:** None firm. Watch NCT07247175 for results and for any newly registered powered, patient-outcome RCT.

### TRIG-007 — JEDEC/HBM roadmap reviving near-term 2029-relevant D2W hybrid-bonding demand
(a) **Status:** NOT met (demand is being further POSTPONED, not pulled forward). JEDEC raised the HBM4 package-height cap to **775 µm (JESD270-4, published Apr 16, 2025)**, allowing microbumps/TC bonding to continue. In 2026, industry/press reporting indicates JEDEC is considering **further relaxation to ~825-900 µm for HBM4E**, which would further **delay** hybrid bonding. Industry consensus places full-scale HBM hybrid bonding at the **HBM5 (20-high) generation around 2029-2030** — Counterpoint Research (Apr 6, 2026, via Seoul Economic Daily): "SK hynix is expected to launch HBM5 around 2029–2030 in line with the next-generation AI GPU cycle, and hybrid bonding will enter full-scale mass production from that point." No 2026 roadmap change creates *near-term* (pre-2029) direct D2W demand.
(b) **Source:** Semiconductor Engineering, "HBM4 Sticks With Microbumps, Postponing Hybrid Bonding" (notes JEDEC revised module height 720→775 µm); TrendForce (Apr 1, 2026) on the ~900 µm consideration; Counterpoint Research via Seoul Economic Daily (Apr 6, 2026); JEDEC JESD270-4 press release (Apr 16, 2025).
(c) **Classification:** speculative (no near-term pull; the trend is delay)
(d) **Confidence:** high overall; **medium specifically on the ~825-900 µm figure**, which is press-reported (TrendForce citing Newsis/Chosun Ilbo), not yet a published JEDEC standard
(e) **Change vs as-of:** The postponement thesis is **reinforced**, not reversed. The 2026 ~825-900 µm discussions push hybrid bonding further out, contrary to a near-term pull. (Caveat: NVIDIA could force an earlier timeline if it imposes leap-forward Rubin/Feynman requirements; Samsung is sampling 16-Hi HBM4 hybrid-bonded parts to NVIDIA at reportedly ~10% yield — a watch item, not a fired trigger.)
(f) **Next milestone:** HBM4E standard finalization (height decision) and HBM5 ramp (~2029-2030) are the inflection points. RID-041/RID-042 stay killed/on watch.

FDA issued a **March 18, 2026 DRAFT guidance**, "General Considerations for the Use of New Approach Methodologies in Drug Development" (FR Doc 2026-05390, Docket FDA-2025-D-6131; comments due **May 18, 2026**) — explicitly draft, framework-only, and does **not** mandate OoC acceptance in lieu of animal models. **FDA Modernization Act 3.0:** Senate **S.355 passed by unanimous consent on Dec 16, 2025** (with amendment SA 4057); House companion **H.R. 2821** (lead sponsor Rep. ISTAND remains the qualification pathway (Emulate Liver-Chip accepted; FDA accepted a first in silico DDT on June 3, 2026), but **no general regulatory acceptance of OoC data in lieu of animal models for drug submissions** has been finalized.
(b) **Source:** Federal Register FR 2026-05390 (https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2026/03/19/2026-05390/); FDA press release (Mar 18, 2026); Congress.gov S.355 (https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/senate-bill/355, "Passed Senate with an amendment by Unanimous Consent") and H.R.2821 (https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/house-bill/2821); FDA ISTAND program page.
(c) **Classification:** active (clear regulatory momentum; no final, binding action yet creating direct procurement/qualification demand)
(d) **Confidence:** high
(e) **Change vs as-of:** Material progress vs your as-of facts. New **March 2026 draft NAM guidance** issued, and **FDA Mod Act 3.0 advanced in the House** after Senate passage. Still permissive/aspirational and implementation-oriented, **not** a procurement-forcing mandate (the law removes the animal-testing default and aligns regulations; it is "not a blanket prohibition on animal studies").
(f) **Next milestone:** Finalization of the NAM guidance (after the May 18, 2026 comment close) and a full House vote on H.R. 2821 — either would be the concrete trigger; neither has occurred. RID-086 stays on "watch," now with upgraded watch intensity.
