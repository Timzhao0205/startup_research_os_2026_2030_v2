## (D) Narrative Summary
Of the 16 placeholder RIDs: **8 CLEARED, 7 CLEARED_WITH_WEAKNESS, 1 HOLD.**

All three priority "pursue" RIDs cleared on direct Tier 1 evidence: **RID-111** (peer-reviewed nanopositioning control literature establishing the nm-precision/kHz-bandwidth tradeoff, plus an advanced-packaging hybrid-bonding 0.2 μm-at-3σ placement spec), **RID-016** (China MIIT echelon-utilization policy + UL 1974 + NREL economics), and **RID-100** (peer-reviewed silicon-photonics review naming active alignment as the assembly bottleneck).

**RID-094 (AM in-situ NDE) is the only HOLD.** A genuine Tier 1 NIST source exists (NIST AM-Bench / 2026 in-situ metrology roadmap, which states "a key barrier is the lack of reliable and validated in situ measurements"), but the RID is a **prior kill** and collides directly with the project's stated aversion to diagnostic/testing/monitoring overproduction. No standalone procurement or standards-driven demand decoupled from monitoring was found, so per the hard rules it is not forced to clear and is routed back to 02c.

**Closest near-miss to retry later: RID-099 (extreme-precision metrology).** It nearly went HOLD on the same monitoring-adjacency concern as RID-094, but a direct, specific Tier 1 driver was found (sub-1 nm overlay accuracy required below the 7 nm node), so it clears with weakness; it should be explicitly re-examined for the testing-overproduction concern at 02e.
